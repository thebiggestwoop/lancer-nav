Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 24, 2026

This policy covers the Lancer Companion Discord bot and its companion Owlbear Rodeo extension (together, "Lancer Companion," "we," or "the service"). It's an unofficial, free, hobby-run dice-rolling utility for the Lancer tabletop RPG.

Information we collect

Through the Discord bot:

  • Message content, but only for messages that start with the bot's command prefix (l!) — used solely to parse and execute that command.
  • Your Discord user ID, username/display name, and server/channel IDs — used to reply to you, attribute a roll to you in the shared activity log, and route pairing codes to the right channel.

Through the Owlbear extension:

  • Your Owlbear player display name — used to label rolls in Roll History.
  • The dice expressions and results you roll.
  • A pairing code, only if you choose to link a room to a Discord channel.

We don't require or collect an account, email address, or any payment information — there's nothing to sign up for.

How we use it

Solely to operate the features described in the project's README: rolling dice, replying in Discord, and sharing/posting roll results between Owlbear and a linked Discord channel. We don't use your data for advertising, profiling, or anything unrelated to running the bot and extension.

Storage and retention

There is no database. Everything the bot process holds — pairing codes and the per-guild roll activity log (capped at a small number of recent entries) — lives only in server memory and is lost whenever the bot restarts; it is never written to disk or backed up.

Data the extension keeps (saved rolls, your settings, a linked pairing code) lives entirely in your own browser's local storage or in Owlbear's own room metadata sync — it never passes through our server except at the moment a roll needs to be posted to a linked Discord channel.

Third parties

We don't sell, rent, or share your data with anyone. The only other parties involved are the platforms this project runs on top of — Discord and Owlbear Rodeo — each governed by their own privacy policies for the data their platforms process.

Children's privacy

This service is not directed at children. Using Discord at all requires meeting Discord's own minimum age requirement under its Terms of Service.

Your choices

Remove the bot from your server, or simply stop using the extension, to stop any further processing. Clearing your browser's local storage removes all client-side data the extension has kept. Leaving a Discord room unpaired (or turning off "Pair with Discord" in Settings) keeps your rolls entirely local to your own browser.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated occasionally as the project changes. Continuing to use the bot or extension after a change constitutes acceptance of the update.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised by opening an issue on the GitHub repository.
